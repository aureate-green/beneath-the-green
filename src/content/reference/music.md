## Project Idea

Stories can have companion songs: sometimes literal songs, sometimes instrumental vibes, sometimes in-universe recordings, hymns, work songs, metal demos, field recordings, or radio fragments.

Music should not be decoration. It should act like another door into the world.

## Overall Sound

Southern gothic cosmic horror with modern grit.

Possible ingredients:

- doom metal
- sludge metal
- Appalachian folk textures
- dark country / murder ballad influence
- gothic Americana
- blackened folk
- industrial drones
- dark ambient
- field recordings: cicadas, storms, church fans, power lines, frogs, distant trains
- old hymnal melodies warped into minor keys
- banjo, fiddle, bowed guitar, harmonium, pump organ
- low distorted bass, ritual drums, handclaps, chain percussion

## Emotional Targets

- heat lightning over a trailer park
- a funeral procession stopping at a flooded road
- gospel from a church with nobody inside
- doom riff under cicadas
- the feeling of being watched by the tree line
- tenderness in a ruined place
- a voice singing from under the floor

## Possible Companion Track Concepts

### The Storm Cell That Would Not Move
Slow doom / dark ambient. Repeating emergency-alert tones buried under guitar feedback. Vocals like a dispatcher trying to stay calm.

### Waffle House Saint
Dark country with brushed drums and low tremolo guitar. Warm, tired, protective. The chorus should feel like coffee at 3 AM before something walks in.

### Kudzu Gospel
Hymnal melody swallowed by drone and banjo. Choir voices gradually become too numerous and too close.

### The Company Town That Paid Its Dead
Industrial folk / work song. Pickaxes, chains, boot stomps, low male chorus, payroll printer percussion.

### The Girl Who Knew the Tornado's Name
Post-rock / storm folk. Starts small and childlike, builds into rotating walls of sound.

## Rule

If the song sounds generically spooky, it failed.

It should sound like a place.
